Definition

The CSS box model describes how elements are structured and displayed in terms of four components:

  • Content: The actual content of the box (text, images, etc.).
  • Padding: The space between the content and the border; transparent area around the content.
  • Border: A line surrounding the padding (if any) and content.
  • Margin: The outermost space around the box, creating distance between it and other elements.
